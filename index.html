Pipe Stress Engineering

[ SECTION 04 ]

Focused on advancing expertise in pipe stress analysis — the critical discipline ensuring piping systems withstand thermal expansion, sustained loads, and dynamic forces throughout their operational lifecycle.

[ 01 ]

Flexibility Analysis

Evaluating piping system flexibility per ASME B31.3 to ensure safe thermal expansion and contraction without excessive stress on equipment nozzles or supports.

[ 02 ]

Sustained Load Evaluation

Analyzing weight-induced stresses including pipe, fluid, insulation, and component loads to verify structural integrity under static conditions.

[ 03 ]

Thermal Expansion Analysis

Modeling temperature-induced displacement and stress cycles to design effective expansion loops, offsets, and flexible routing configurations.

[ 04 ]

Nozzle Load Verification

Validating forces and moments at equipment connections against API, NEMA, and manufacturer limits to protect rotating and static equipment.

[ 05 ]

Pulsation Analysis

Assessing acoustic-induced vibration and flow-induced pulsation in compressor and pump discharge systems per API 618/619 standards.

[ 06 ]

Pipe Support Optimization

Designing and optimizing support locations, types, and spring selections to control displacement while minimizing stress concentrations.
